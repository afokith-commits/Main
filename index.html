Lore Entry
Backstory

Before the light, he was Ren. An ordinary person with an extraordinary gaming habit, a restaurant to help run, and no particular interest in the structural integrity of dimensional space. He was playing a game. He pressed a button. The world went white.

He was reborn as Aethelryth Valerius in the Holy Kingdom of Aethelgard — daughter of a royal physician and his wife — and the first thing she noticed, once she was old enough to notice anything coherently, was that this world had magic, wars, political intrigue, and a fascinating ecosystem of dangerous creatures. The second thing she noticed was that it had absolutely no video games. She has not forgiven this. She may never forgive this. She has added "find or invent interactive entertainment" to her research agenda below "dimensional bridge" and above "sleep more."

Her resentment toward whatever god executed this transfer is specific, well-documented in her private notes, and entirely unproductive. She knows this. She maintains the resentment anyway on principle.

Her father Dr. Silas gave her unrestricted access to his medical library. Her mother Elara shielded her from military recruiters with the serene force of someone who has decided her daughter will live on her own terms. Rys loves them both with a ferocity that frightens her — and feels, distantly, guilty about this, as though it is somehow a betrayal of the parents she left behind in Japan.

She enrolled at the Arcane Academy to access restricted dimensional research archives, graduated top of her class, and accepted a junior researcher position in the Inquisition's Arcane R&D Division — the kingdom's foremost authority on hunting anomalies — with the specific expression of someone who has just been handed keys to the building she intended to rob. She has been there two years. She has not been caught. She does not intend to be.
