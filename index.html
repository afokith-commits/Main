Othellion didn't join the Demon Lord because he loved darkness. He joined because he was a high-ranking minister in the Mortal Kingdoms who tried to launch a massive, continent-spanning welfare and infrastructure project — and watched it be devoured from within.
Project Designation · Classified Initiative
Proyek MBG — Mana Bagi Gratis

A continent-spanning program designed to distribute residual mana evenly across the peasant class, ensuring that no common person would starve, freeze, or die for lack of arcane sustenance. Drafted over 50 years. Approved. Then immediately cannibalized.

  • Spent 50 years drafting a continent-spanning welfare framework. Budget approved by the King. Optimism at an all-time high.
  • The moment the ink dried, fellow ministers circled like ravens — elbowing him out of every committee, installing their own kin as project directors within the week.
  • Contracted resources for the peasant tier were marked up 400% by regional barons before ever leaving the capital.
  • The "Free Mana" that reached common hands was toxic, low-grade residue — the premium allocations had already been redirected to line elite pockets.
    Corruption Confirmed
  • When Othellion attempted to file a formal audit and blow the whistle, his colleagues produced fabricated evidence, charged him with treason, and had him exiled before the week was out. The Mortal Kingdoms weren't merely flawed. They were a systemic cancer of institutional rot.
"You want to build your perfect, balanced world? I will give you the raw authority and absolute obedience to do it. No committees. No corrupt barons. No backstabbing. Just execution."
— The Demon Lord, Offer of Appointment

Othellion accepted. He became the General of the New Order, using the Demon Lord's iron fist to force-implement his resource-balancing policies across occupied territories. Cities that refused to optimize mana consumption weren't argued with. Their elites were liquidated via shadow-beasts inside 72 hours.

His personal justification, recorded in his private audit logs: "At least under the Demon Lord, the projects actually get finished on time." He became the very tyrant he used to despise. He just convinced himself it was for efficiency.

As the Hero Party breaches the innermost walls, Othellion surveys the wreckage of the Demon Lord's administration and arrives at a deeply humiliating conclusion.

  • The other Demon Generals had been skimming Anima from the frontlines the entire war.
  • Casualty reports were systematically falsified to unlock additional command-budget disbursements.
  • The Demon Lord's empire didn't fall to the heroes. It collapsed from within — from the exact same administrative rot that destroyed the Mortal Kingdoms.
